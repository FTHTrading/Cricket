## What the Prior Thesis Claimed

The prior version of this repository presented the following narrative:

- FutureTech was building or acquiring a cricket stadium in the Atlanta metro area
- The stadium would be owned and operated by FTH as the primary asset
- A tokenized securities offering would be structured around stadium revenue and appreciation
- The $150M capital stack (see `archived-capital-stack.md`) would fund stadium acquisition and build-out
- Token holders would participate in stadium revenue distributions

---

## Why This Framing Was Retired

### 1. Ownership Claims Were Not Accurate
FutureTech does not own a cricket stadium. It does not have a signed agreement to acquire one. Characterizing the company as a stadium owner before ownership is established creates a misrepresentation of material fact.

### 2. Commercial Rights Were Assumed, Not Secured
The prior thesis built revenue and token distribution projections around commercial rights — naming, hospitality, media, F&B — that FTH does not currently hold. Rights must be secured before they can be monetized or sold to investors.

### 3. Securities Framing Was Premature
Describing an active or imminent securities offering without legal review, registered offering documents, or investor verification infrastructure creates regulatory exposure under securities anti-fraud rules.

### 4. The Strategy Was Backwards
Building towards tokenization before establishing operating proof was the wrong sequence. The correct sequence is: operate, then monetize, then structure capital. The prior thesis inverted this — tokenization first, operations second.
