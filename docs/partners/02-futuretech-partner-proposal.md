## 1. Executive Summary

FutureTech Holding Company is an existing participant in the Georgia cricket ecosystem. We operate within it through the Atlanta Premier League and FutureTech Lions, and we have a documented history of cricket infrastructure engagement in the Atlanta metro area.

We are seeking commercial and operating partnerships around the next phase of Georgia cricket growth — specifically across league administration, venue commerce, sponsorship systems, membership infrastructure, and community activation.

This proposal describes what FTH can operate, what value it creates for partners, how an engagement could be structured, and what we need to proceed.

We are not proposing to own your venue or control your league. We are proposing to build and operate the systems that make your operation more commercially effective from day one.

## 4. Why League and Venue Layers Must Be Connected

The mistake most cricket operators make in the U.S. is treating the league and the venue as separate commercial problems.

They are not. They are the same demand base moving through different monetization points.

**A player who registers in a league is also:**
- a ticketing customer for venue events
- a potential premium member
- a youth program parent
- a referral source for corporate sponsors who want to reach the community

**A venue event produces:**
- attendance data that makes sponsorship measurable
- transaction data that validates membership ROI
- hospitality demand that supports premium product pricing
- community integration data that de-risks capital structures when the time comes

Connecting the league and the venue into a single operating infrastructure creates a data flywheel that makes every commercial product more valuable over time.

FTH's platform is built around this connection. It is not two separate products bolted together — it is one commercial engine with league-facing and venue-facing interfaces.

## 6. How FutureTech Creates Value Without Claiming Venue Ownership

The clearest statement of our commercial proposition:

> **FTH brings systems. Partners bring rights. The commercial product comes from combining them.**

FTH does not need to own the venue to operate the Venue OS. FTH does not need to control the league to run the League OS. The value comes from the operating agreement — a contract that defines the product FTH delivers, the fees FTH earns, and the data each party retains.

This is a standard SaaS and managed-services commercial model. It does not require equity transfer, venue title, or league control.

**What FTH earns:**
- platform subscription or revenue share fees from the venue operator
- league software fees from the league organization
- percentage of transaction volume processed through payment rails
- setup and implementation fees for new modules
- sponsor reporting platform fees
- optional participation in premium product commercial structures

**What the partner retains:**
- venue ownership and operations control
- league governance and decision-making authority
- commercial relationships with sponsors, media, and naming-rights partners
- all data generated within the platform (under agreed data governance terms)

## 8. 90-Day Partner Launch Plan

### Phase A — Alignment and Diligence (Days 1–30)

- Introductory meeting with venue and/or league leadership
- FTH maps existing operating roles and confirms where platform access begins
- Partner identifies priority operating gaps (ticketing? sponsorship? registration? settlement?)
- FTH delivers module overview and proposes pilot scope
- Legal framework for operating agreement initiated

### Phase B — Commercial Architecture (Days 20–50)

- Define fee structures and revenue share terms for proposed modules
- Define data ownership and access terms
- Agree on pilot module scope and success metrics
- Agree on integration requirements with any existing systems
- Draft operating agreement or letter of intent

### Phase C — Pilot Build and Launch (Days 45–90)

- FTH configures pilot module (sponsorship reporting, event settlement, membership wallet, or league registration)
- Partner tests and approves system in staging environment
- Live launch at next eligible event or season start
- Post-launch reporting delivered to partner within 48 hours of event close
- Review and decision on full platform engagement
