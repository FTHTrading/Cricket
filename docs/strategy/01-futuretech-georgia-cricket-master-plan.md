## 3. Metro Market Structure

The Georgia cricket market is demographically driven by immigration patterns from South Asia, the Caribbean, and East Africa. This is not a developing market — it has a mature participation base operating without adequate infrastructure.

### Metropolitan Cricket Corridor

| Zone | Characteristics | Opportunity |
|------|-----------------|-------------|
| Gwinnett County | Largest South Asian population concentration in Georgia; active club and league cricket | League OS anchor market; near-term member enrollment |
| Forsyth County | Fast-growing cricket participation; multiple informal clubs | League expansion; academy targets |
| N.Fulton / Johns Creek | Established cricket with higher household income concentration | Premium membership; sponsor market |
| Peachtree Corners / Norcross | FTH home base; diverse professional community | Headquarters credibility; member base |
| South Atlanta / DeKalb | Caribbean cricket community; longer U.S. cricket history | Community activation; tournament anchoring |
| LaGrange / Troup County | Anchor regional venue opportunity; state-funded and private development interest | Venue OS deployment target |

### What the Market Needs

The metro market currently has:
- participants without a unified registration and account system
- leagues without professional-grade operating software
- sponsors without measurable performance data
- events without integrated settlement and reporting
- no premium membership or hospitality infrastructure to speak of

This is not a gap that requires inventing demand. It requires building supply.

## 4. Venue Opportunity Logic

### LaGrange Cricket Grounds

LaGrange Cricket Grounds is the most concrete venue catalyst in the Georgia market. Public documentation indicates:
- Cricket-first design with broadcast-ready infrastructure
- Groundbreaking: November 6, 2025
- Target opening: Q1 2027 (subject to approvals and programs)
- Active solicitation of strategic partners in investment, naming rights, and programming
- Positioned as a regional economic development asset, not just a sports facility

**What that creates for FTH:**

LaGrange is explicitly seeking partners. FTH is explicitly a cricket operator with systems capacity. The match is direct — the question is what role FTH proposes.

FTH should **not** propose ownership. FTH should propose becoming the operating infrastructure and commercialization partner that makes LaGrange's business model work from opening day:
- ticketing systems
- member and pass programs
- sponsor inventory and reporting
- concession and vendor settlement
- premium box and suite commerce
- event settlement and financial reporting

A venue without operating infrastructure loses revenue on every event. FTH offers to close that gap.

### Venue Partner Logic (General)

Even absent LaGrange, the Georgia market has a persistent need: existing cricket grounds, parks, and fields host events with no commercial infrastructure. Every park-and-pay tournament leaves money on the table. FTH can build the systems that collect it.

## 6. Payments and Infrastructure as the Operating Wedge

Payments are the highest-leverage entry point into any operating relationship. Every revenue category listed above runs through a payment event. The operator who owns the payment infrastructure owns the data, the audit trail, the settlement, and the relationship.

FTH's payments and settlement capability is not a downstream feature — it is the core wedge.

**What payments infrastructure enables:**
- real-time revenue reporting for venue and league operators
- sponsor performance measurement linked to actual transactions
- member account management with recurring billing
- event-day settlement and reconciliation
- commission and revenue-share automation for vendors
- clean audit trail for any future finance structuring

The sponsor reporting module, the membership wallet, the vendor settlement system, and the league payment rails are all payment-adjacent. Building them creates the operating data layer that makes every other commercial product more valuable.

## 8. Risks and Mitigants

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
|------|----------------|------------|
| Venue dependence | LaGrange is not yet open; delay shifts the venue OS deployment timeline | Build League OS in parallel; do not sequence everything after LaGrange opening |
| Partnership ambiguity | Conversations with APL, venue, and clubs may not formalize quickly | Structure pilots around existing FTH footprint; build with or without external partners initially |
| Rights clarity | Commercial rights at APL may not be fully documented | Legal review of APL operating agreements before building commercial products on APL foundation |
| Competition | National cricket operators or sports tech platforms may enter | Speed to market and local relationship advantage are the moat; move in 2025–2026 window |
| Adoption resistance | Leagues and clubs may resist adopting new systems | Pilot one module at a time; minimize disruption; offer integrations alongside existing tools |
| League fragmentation | Georgia cricket has multiple competing leagues and bodies | Build neutral infrastructure that serves all leagues; avoid being perceived as APL-exclusive |
| Capital timeline | Finance structures require data and rights that take 18–24 months to establish | Maintain strict phase sequencing; do not advance capital work before operating data exists |
| Securities risk | Any capital markets work that is prematurely executed creates legal exposure | No securities offering without securities counsel engagement and full gating compliance |

## 10. Long-Term Expansion Logic

The Georgia market is the proof-of-concept. A functional League OS and Venue OS in Georgia creates a replicable template for:

- other Sun Belt cricket markets (Texas, Florida, North Carolina, Virginia)
- nationally organized U.S. cricket league infrastructure
- international cricket market entry via diaspora-driven U.S. cities
- a scalable SaaS operating model for cricket league and venue operators

The capital markets layer, when activated, is not Georgia-specific. ERC-3643-compliant preferred equity and SPV structures for premium cricket real estate and hospitality assets are replicable across every market that reaches the operating maturity criteria.

The long-term outcome: FutureTech as the operating infrastructure layer for U.S. cricket — starting from the position it already holds in Georgia.
